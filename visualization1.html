National Overview of Road Safety Enforcement in Australia (2008-2024)

This page provides a national-level overview of traffic law enforcement across Australia, based on data from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE). The visualisations below allow users to explore how penalties such as fines, charges, and arrests vary across different states and over time.

Use the filters above to select specific years, penalty types, violation types, detection methods, age groups and jurisdiction. Hover over areas or lines for detailed statistics, and use the "Reset Zoom" button to return to the default view.

Traffic Penalties by State and Territory

From the map above, it is evident that New South Wales, Queensland, and Victoria show higher penalty counts compared to smaller states such as Tasmania and the Northern Territory. This could reflect both larger populations and greater enforcement resources.

Yearly Trend of Traffic Penalties (2008-2024)

Overall, the total number of penalties across Australia shows a moderate fluctuation from 2008 to 2024, with peaking at 2021 and a gradual decline in recent years. The data suggests that enforcement activity has remained consistent nationwide, with no extreme increases or decreases observed over the long term.
It is important to note that data for arrests and charges is only available for 2023 and 2024. Therefore, long-term comparisons for these categories are not included in this trend analysis. Earlier years mainly contain fine-related records collected under broader enforcement reporting methods.

Data sourced from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE)
