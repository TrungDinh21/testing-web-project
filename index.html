Enforcing Fairness: Visualizing Road Safety Enforcement Across Australia (2008–2024)

Interactive visualisations to explore whether enforcement (fines, charges, arrests) is applied fairly across Australian jurisdictions.

Get to know us

Background & Motivation

This project focuses on the enforcement of road safety laws across Australia (2008-2024), using data provided by the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE).
The aim is to investigate whether enforcement activities such as fines, charges and arrests are applied equitably across states and territories after accounting for licence counts.

Target users: policymakers, researchers, journalists and the public. The visualisations support transparency and data-driven discussion about enforcement fairness and policy.

Quick facts

  • Time span: 2008–2024
  • Main source: BITRE Road Safety Enforcement
  • Normalization: Penalties per 10,000 driver licences
  • Pages:
    1. National Overview
    2. State Trends
    3. Fairness Comparison

Purpose & Key Questions

The primary goal of this project is to reveal how enforcement varies by time, jurisdiction and demographic group, and to allow fair interstate comparisons by normalising counts by driver licences.

Key questions

  • How has enforcement changed over time?
  • Which jurisdictions enforce most strictly (absolute and per-licence)?
  • Which offence types and detection methods are most prevalent?
  • How do penalties differ across age groups?
