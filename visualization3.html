Fairness Comparison of Road Safety Enforcement Across Australia

This page enables users to compare the fairness of road safety enforcement among Australian jurisdictions. The visualisations below present both total penalties and penalties per 10,000 driver licences, helping users determine whether variations in enforcement are due to stricter policing or larger driving populations.

Use the filters above to select specific years, violation types, detection methods, and age groups. The bar charts update dynamically based on your selections. Hovering over any bar reveals detailed statistics on fines, charges, and arrests.

Penalties per 10,000 Driver Licences

Total Penalties by Jurisdiction

When adjusted for population size, smaller jurisdictions such as the Northern Territory often display higher per-licence penalty rates. This may reflect stricter enforcement practices, demographic factors, or differences in camera placement and policing resources.

In contrast, Victoria, Queensland, and New South Wales consistently show higher absolute penalty counts, which is expected given their larger populations and extensive enforcement networks. Comparing absolute and normalised values helps reveal jurisdictions with proportionally higher or lower enforcement intensity.

Together, these visualisations provide a balanced and transparent assessment of road safety enforcement across Australia. By presenting two complementary perspectives, the Fairness Comparison page supports critical and meaningful interpretation of policing practices nationwide.

The dataset includes several limitations that may affect interpretation. Manual enforcement speeding data is incomplete prior to 2016. Unlicensed driving and age group categories are mainly recorded from 2023–2024. Variations in population, data coverage, and the number of detection cameras across states influence penalty counts. Therefore, figures should be viewed as indicative rather than directly comparable across jurisdictions.

Data sourced from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE)
